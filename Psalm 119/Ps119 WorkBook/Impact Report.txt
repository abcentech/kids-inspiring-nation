## INTRODUCTION: WHAT THIS REPORT IS

### Welcome to Your Final Report!

This is your opportunity to tell the complete story of your National Builders project. The judges will read this report to decide if you're one of the **7-10 finalists** who will compete for **₦3,000,000 in prizes** at the Grand Finale.

**What judges are looking for:**

✅ **REAL IMPACT** - Did you actually help people?  
✅ **VALUES DEMONSTRATED** - Did you live the 8 National Builder values?  
✅ **RESOURCEFULNESS** - Did you solve problems creatively with limited resources?  
✅ **HONESTY** - Is your story truthful and authentic?  
✅ **SUSTAINABILITY** - Will your solution continue?  

**This report is NOT about:**
- Fancy language or big words
- Exaggerating your impact
- Copying from the internet
- Making yourself look perfect

**This report IS about:**
- Telling your honest story
- Showing real evidence of impact
- Demonstrating values in action
- What you learned along the way

### HOW LONG SHOULD IT BE?

**Maximum: 15 pages** (not counting photos, receipts, appendices)

**Recommended structure:**
- Basic info & Problem: 2-3 pages
- Solution & Implementation: 4-5 pages
- Impact & Results: 3-4 pages
- Values, Learning, Sustainability: 3-4 pages
- Financial report: 1-2 pages

**You can write LESS if your story is clear. Quality over quantity!**

## REPORT STRUCTURE & GUIDELINES

### WRITING TIPS

**DO:**
- Write in simple, clear language
- Be specific (use numbers, names, dates)
- Tell stories about real people
- Include quotes from beneficiaries
- Admit challenges and failures
- Show your personality
- Use active voice ("We built..." not "A structure was built...")

**DON'T:**
- Use complicated words to sound smart
- Copy-paste from other sources
- Exaggerate or lie about impact
- Write vaguely ("many people" - how many?)
- Focus only on successes (failures teach too!)
- Forget to proofread

### FORMATTING

**Font:** Arial or Times New Roman, size 11-12  
**Spacing:** 1.5 or double-spaced  
**Margins:** 1 inch on all sides  
**Page numbers:** Bottom center  
**Headers:** Include your team name on each page  

### LANGUAGE

You may write in:
- English
- Pidgin English
- Hausa
- Yoruba
- Igbo

**Choose the language you're most comfortable with!** Judges care about your story, not fancy English.

## VIDEO DOCUMENTARY GUIDELINES

### WHAT IS THE VIDEO?

A 2-3 minute film telling your project story.

**Purpose:**
- Show judges what you did (don't just tell)
- Capture beneficiaries speaking
- Show your personality and values
- Provide visual proof of impact

### VIDEO STRUCTURE (2-3 minutes)

**0:00-0:20 (20 seconds): INTRODUCTION**
- You/your team on camera
- State: Name, project title, location
- Briefly say what problem you solved

**0:20-0:40 (20 seconds): THE PROBLEM**
- Show footage of the problem (before)
- Explain why it mattered

**0:40-1:40 (60 seconds): YOUR SOLUTION**
- Show you/team working
- Show the solution being implemented
- Show progress over time
- Action shots (not just talking heads)

**1:40-2:20 (40 seconds): IMPACT**
- Beneficiaries speaking (testimonials)
- Show the change (before/after)
- Numbers on screen (e.g., "50 families helped")

**2:20-2:40 (20 seconds): VALUES & CLOSING**
- State which values guided you
- What you learned
- Thank the community
- Your contact for replication

**2:40-3:00 (20 seconds): CREDITS (optional)**
- Team members' names
- Partners/donors acknowledged
- NBC logo

---

### VIDEO TECHNICAL REQUIREMENTS

**Length:** 2-3 minutes (absolute maximum: 4 minutes)

**Format:** MP4, MOV, or AVI

**Quality:** Smartphone quality is fine (minimum 720p)

**Sound:** Clear audio (minimize background noise)

**File size:** Under 500MB (compress if needed)

---

### FILMING TIPS

**CONTENT:**
- Show, don't just tell
- Include real beneficiaries speaking
- Be authentic (don't script everything)
- Show your team in action
- Include before/after comparisons

**TECHNICAL:**
- Film horizontally (landscape, not portrait)
- Hold phone steady (use both hands or lean against something)
- Film in good lighting (outdoors or near windows)
- Get close to people when they speak
- Record clean audio (minimize wind/traffic noise)

**EDITING:**
- Keep it simple (you don't need fancy effects)
- Add text on screen for key numbers
- Include subtitles if audio is hard to hear
- Use free apps: CapCut, InShot, iMovie
- Add simple background music (optional, but keep it low)

---

### WHAT TO AVOID

❌ Too long (judges won't watch 10-minute videos)  
❌ Just you talking (show action!)  
❌ Poor audio (people can't hear what's being said)  
❌ All "after" with no "before" (can't see the change)  
❌ No beneficiaries speaking (their voices matter most)  
❌ Too much text on screen (keep it minimal)
