## VALUE 1: INTEGRITY & TRUTH-SEEKING

### WHAT IT MEANS

**Simple Definition:**  
Always tell the truth. Do what's right even when no one is watching.

**Nigerian Translations:**
- **Pidgin:** "I go do am well, no shortcut."
- **Hausa:** "Gaskiya take kawai."
- **Yoruba:** "Otitọ ni apakan mi."
- **Igbo:** "Eziokwu bụ ụzọ m."

### WHY IT MATTERS

Nigeria struggles with dishonesty everywhere:
- Students cheat on exams
- Business people sell fake products
- Politicians make false promises
- People lie to get ahead

This is why many Nigerians don't trust each other. This is why corruption hurts our nation.

If you want to build a better Nigeria, you must be different. You must choose **TRUTH** over lies, even when lies would be easier.

### NIGERIAN HEROES WHO SHOWED INTEGRITY

#### 1. PROF. DORA AKUNYILI (1954-2014)

As Director-General of NAFDAC, she fought fake drugs in Nigeria. Powerful drug dealers threatened to kill her. They offered her millions in bribes.

But Professor Akunyili refused. She said, *"I will not compromise my integrity, even if it costs me my life."*

Because of her integrity, millions of Nigerians were saved from dangerous fake medicines.

#### 2. GANI FAWEHINMI (1938-2009)

This fearless lawyer stood for justice his entire life. He defended poor people for free. He spoke truth to corrupt military leaders who could have killed him.

He was arrested many times. He was beaten. But he never stopped telling the truth.

Gani Fawehinmi showed us that integrity means standing for what's right, no matter the cost.

### INTEGRITY IN EVERYDAY LIFE

Let's look at real situations you might face:

#### EXAMPLE 1: The Lost Money

You find ₦500 on the ground at school. No one saw you pick it up. What do you do?

**❌ DISHONEST CHOICE:**  
Keep it for yourself. Buy snacks. Tell no one.

**✅ INTEGRITY CHOICE:**  
Ask around to find who lost it. If no one claims it, give it to a teacher to announce. If still no one claims it after some days, then maybe you can keep it - but you tried to do right.

**WHY INTEGRITY MATTERS HERE:**  
Someone might desperately need that money. By being honest, you might help a friend who lost their lunch money or school fees.

---

#### EXAMPLE 2: The Copied Homework

Your friend copied your homework word-for-word. The teacher thinks it's their original work and praises them. What do you do?

**❌ DISHONEST CHOICE:**  
Stay quiet. It's not your problem.

**✅ INTEGRITY CHOICE:**  
Talk to your friend privately: *"I'm glad I could help, but you need to do your own work. If you don't understand, let's study together."*

If they keep copying, tell them you won't share your work anymore.

**WHY INTEGRITY MATTERS HERE:**  
Your friend won't learn if they keep copying. You're actually helping them by encouraging honesty.

---

#### EXAMPLE 3: The Project Mistake

You made a mistake in your National Builders project report. You said you helped 100 people, but you counted wrong - it was only 50 people. You already submitted the report. What do you do?

**❌ DISHONEST CHOICE:**  
Leave the lie in the report. Hope no one notices.

**✅ INTEGRITY CHOICE:**  
Contact your coordinator immediately: *"I made a mistake in my report. The real number is 50, not 100. I'm sorry for the error."*

**WHY INTEGRITY MATTERS HERE:**  
Honesty builds trust. Judges will respect you MORE for admitting a mistake than if they discover you lied.

## VALUE 2: DISCIPLINE & DILIGENCE

### WHAT IT MEANS

**Simple Definition:**  
Work hard and don't give up. Show up every day. Finish what you start.

**Nigerian Translations:**
- **Pidgin:** "I no dey give up; I dey work steady-steady."
- **Hausa:** "Jajircewa da ƙwazo."
- **Yoruba:** "Ìfọkànsìn àti ìgbéhanú."
- **Igbo:** "Ịdị uchu na ịrụsi ọrụ ike."

### WHY IT MATTERS

Many projects in Nigeria fail because people start strong but don't finish. They lose interest. They get distracted. They give up when things get hard.

National Builders are different - we are **disciplined**. We keep going even when it's hard.

Think about:
- A farmer who plants but never waters the crops
- A student who starts studying but quits after one day
- A business that opens with excitement but closes after one month

**Without discipline, nothing great is ever achieved.**

### NIGERIAN HEROES WHO SHOWED DISCIPLINE

#### 1. ALIKO DANGOTE

Started with a small trading business borrowing ₦500,000 from his uncle. Worked every single day for 40+ years. Never gave up through economic crashes, competition, and challenges.

Today, he's Africa's richest man - not because of luck, but because of **DISCIPLINE**.

#### 2. CHIOMA AJUNWA

Olympic gold medalist in long jump (1996). She trained every single day for years. When others went to parties, she trained. When it rained, she trained. When she was tired, she trained.

Discipline won her the gold medal for Nigeria.

### DISCIPLINE IN EVERYDAY LIFE

#### EXAMPLE 1: The Saturday Commitment

Your project requires you to visit the community every Saturday for 3 months. It's your friend's birthday party on a Saturday. What do you do?

**❌ LAZY CHOICE:**  
Skip your project work. Go to the party. You can catch up later (but you probably won't).

---

**❌ LAZY CHOICE:**

**✅ DISCIPLINE CHOICE:**  
Read first (30 minutes), then watch TV as your reward.

---

#### EXAMPLE 3: The Monday Meeting

**❌ LAZY CHOICE:**  
Send an excuse message. Stay home.

**✅ DISCIPLINE CHOICE:**  
Go to the meeting even if you're tired. Your team is counting on you.

## VALUE 3: WISDOM & DISCERNMENT

### WHAT IT MEANS

**Simple Definition:**  
Think carefully before you act. Seek advice from wise people. Learn from mistakes.

**Nigerian Translations:**
- **Pidgin:** "I sabi think am well before I do."
- **Hausa:** "Hikima da basira."
- **Yoruba:** "Ọgbọn àti ìmọ̀ràn."
- **Igbo:** "Amamihe na nghọta."

### WHY IT MATTERS

Many problems in Nigeria come from poor decisions:
- Choosing leaders who don't care about citizens
- Starting businesses without planning
- Building houses in flood zones
- Dropping out of school for quick money
- Trusting scammers who promise easy wealth

**Wisdom means making SMART choices, not just FAST choices.**

### NIGERIAN EXAMPLE: WISDOM VS. FOOLISHNESS

**THE FOOLISH BUILDER:**  
In 2012, a man in Lagos built a house in a flood-prone area because the land was cheap. He ignored warnings from neighbors. When the rains came, his house flooded. He lost everything.

**THE WISE BUILDER:**  
His neighbor paid more for land on higher ground. Yes, it cost more. But when the floods came, his house was safe. Twenty years later, that house is still standing.

**The difference?** One acted impulsively. The other sought counsel and thought ahead.

### WISDOM IN EVERYDAY LIFE

#### EXAMPLE 1: The Failed Project

You want to start your National Builders project but don't know how.

**❌ FOOLISH:**  
Just start randomly and hope it works. If it fails, give up.

**✅ WISE:**  
Ask someone who has done something similar. Research online. Make a plan. Test a small version first.

---

#### EXAMPLE 2: Learning from Failure

Your first project idea failed completely. You wasted time and materials.

**❌ FOOLISH:**  
Give up and say *"It's impossible. I can't do this."*

**✅ WISE:**  
Ask *"What can I learn from this failure?"* List what went wrong. Try differently.

Thomas Edison failed 1,000 times before inventing the light bulb. Someone asked, "How did it feel to fail 1,000 times?" He said, "I didn't fail 1,000 times. I successfully discovered 1,000 ways that don't work."

---

#### EXAMPLE 3: Conflicting Advice

Two people give you opposite advice about your project.

**❌ FOOLISH:**  
Just pick one randomly. Or ignore both and do what you feel like.

**✅ WISE:**  
Ask a third person. Weigh the pros and cons of each option. Think about long-term consequences. Pray or meditate on it.

## VALUE 4: SERVICE & COMPASSION

### WHAT IT MEANS

**Simple Definition:**  
Care about other people's problems. Help without expecting anything back.

**Nigerian Translations:**
- **Pidgin:** "My brother's problem na my problem."
- **Hausa:** "Taimako da tausayi."
- **Yoruba:** "Ìràn lọ́wọ́ àti àánú."
- **Igbo:** "Enyemaka na ọmịiko."

### WHY IT MATTERS

Nigeria will only change when we stop being selfish. When we care for our neighbors. When we sacrifice for others.

Jesus said: *"Love your neighbor as yourself."*  
The Quran says: *"None of you truly believes until he loves for his brother what he loves for himself."*

Every religion teaches **SERVICE**. Every great nation was built by people who **SERVED** others.

National Builders serve their communities with compassion.

### THE STORY OF THE GOOD SAMARITAN (Updated for Nigeria)

A man was traveling from Lagos to Ibadan. Armed robbers attacked him, beat him, stole his money, and left him bleeding by the roadside.

A wealthy politician drove past. He saw the bleeding man but said, *"Not my problem,"* and kept driving.

A pastor drove past. He saw the man but thought, *"If I stop, I'll be late for church,"* and kept driving.

Finally, a poor okada rider stopped. He helped the wounded man onto his bike, took him to a clinic, paid for treatment with his own money, and stayed with him until he recovered.

**Who was the hero?** The okada rider who had compassion and served.

### SERVICE IN EVERYDAY LIFE

#### EXAMPLE 1: The Struggling Woman

You see an old woman struggling to carry a heavy bucket of water.

**❌ SELFISH:**  
Walk past. Not your problem.

**✅ SERVICE:**  
Stop and help her carry it to her house. It costs you 5 minutes but means everything to her.

---

#### EXAMPLE 2: The Confused Classmate

A classmate doesn't understand the math lesson. They look frustrated.

**❌ SELFISH:**  
*"That's not my problem. They should have paid attention."*

**✅ SERVICE:**  
*"Hey, do you want me to explain it? I understand it and can help."*

---

#### EXAMPLE 3: The Dirty Community

Your community church/mosque is dirty and needs cleaning.

**❌ SELFISH:**  
Wait for someone else to do it.

**✅ SERVICE:**  
Volunteer your time. Organize friends to help. Lead by example.

## VALUE 5: JUSTICE & RIGHTEOUSNESS

### WHAT IT MEANS

**Simple Definition:**  
Stand for what's right. Treat everyone fairly. Defend those who can't defend themselves.

**Nigerian Translations:**
- **Pidgin:** "E must fair for everybody."
- **Hausa:** "Adalci da gaskiya."
- **Yoruba:** "Ìdájọ́ òdodo àti òtítọ́."
- **Igbo:** "Ikpe ziri ezi na ezi omume."

### WHY IT MATTERS

Nigeria suffers from deep injustice:
- Rich people get away with crimes, poor people go to jail
- Government officials steal public money meant for schools and hospitals
- Some tribes/religions are favored over others
- Women are treated as less than men in many places
- Children from poor families don't get the same opportunities

**National Builders stand for JUSTICE.** We believe:
- Every Nigerian deserves fair treatment
- Every child deserves education
- Every person deserves dignity
- Wrong is wrong, no matter who does it
- Right is right, no matter who opposes it

### NIGERIAN HEROES WHO STOOD FOR JUSTICE

#### 1. BEKO RANSOME-KUTI (1940-2006)

Medical doctor and human rights activist. He fought against military dictatorships. He was arrested over 20 times. He was detained without trial. But he never stopped fighting for justice for ordinary Nigerians.

He said: *"If you see injustice and do nothing, you become part of the injustice."*

#### 2. OKONJO-IWEALA

As Finance Minister, she fought corruption in Nigeria. She exposed officials stealing billions. She created systems to track government spending. Powerful people threatened her and even kidnapped her mother. But she didn't stop fighting for financial justice.

### JUSTICE IN EVERYDAY LIFE

#### EXAMPLE 1: The Unfair Teacher

Your teacher always gives good grades to rich students and bad grades to poor students, even when the poor students' work is better. You're a rich student getting high grades you don't deserve.

**❌ UNJUST:**  
Stay quiet. Enjoy your high grades.

**✅ JUST:**  
Speak privately to the teacher: *"I notice you grade people differently based on their background, not their work. This isn't fair."*

If nothing changes, report to the principal.

---

#### EXAMPLE 2: The Excluded Student

Your group is planning a project. Everyone wants to exclude Amina because she's the only Muslim in the class. You're Christian.

**❌ UNJUST:**  
Go along with the group. *"It's not my decision."*

**✅ JUST:**  
Stand up: *"Amina is just as capable as any of us. Religion shouldn't matter. If she's excluded, I'm not participating either."*

---

#### EXAMPLE 3: The Community Water

The government provides clean water to the rich neighborhood but ignores the poor neighborhood where your project is. Both communities paid taxes.

**❌ UNJUST:**  
Accept it. *"That's just how things are."*

**✅ JUST:**  
Document the inequality. Write to local government. Organize community members to demand fair treatment. Make it a part of your project advocacy.

## VALUE 6: PERSEVERANCE & RESILIENCE

### WHAT IT MEANS

**Simple Definition:**  
Keep going when things get hard. Bounce back from failure. Never give up.

**Nigerian Translations:**
- **Pidgin:** "Wahala no fit stop me."
- **Hausa:** "Juriya da dagewa."
- **Yoruba:** "Ìfarabale àti ipára."
- **Igbo:** "Ntachi obi na imegharị."

### WHY IT MATTERS

**Every great achievement requires perseverance.**

Nelson Mandela spent 27 years in prison but never gave up fighting for freedom.

Thomas Edison failed 10,000 times before inventing the light bulb.

The Chinese Bamboo tree shows no growth for 4 years, then grows 90 feet in 6 weeks - but only because it spent 4 years building deep roots.

**Your National Builders project will face obstacles:**
- Materials will run out
- Team members will disagree
- Community members will doubt you
- You will get tired
- Things will go wrong

**Perseverance is what separates those who finish from those who quit.**

### NIGERIAN HEROES WHO SHOWED PERSEVERANCE

#### 1. WOLE SOYINKA

Nigeria's first Nobel Prize winner. He was imprisoned by military government. His writings were banned. Critics said African literature would never be respected globally.

But he kept writing. He never gave up. In 1986, he won the Nobel Prize in Literature - the first African to do so.

#### 2. THE NIGERIAN PARALYMPIC TEAM

Athletes with disabilities who train with limited resources, no sponsorship, and constant discouragement. People tell them they're wasting their time.

But they persevere. They train in poor conditions. And they win medals for Nigeria at international competitions.

### PERSEVERANCE IN EVERYDAY LIFE

#### EXAMPLE 1: The Difficult Subject

You're failing mathematics. You've tried studying but it's too hard. You want to give up.

**❌ QUITTING:**  
*"I'm just not good at math. I give up."*

**✅ PERSEVERANCE:**  
*"Math is hard for me, but I won't quit. I'll ask my teacher for extra help. I'll find a tutor. I'll practice every day. I'll improve bit by bit."*

---

#### EXAMPLE 2: The Failed Fundraising

Your team tried to raise money for project materials. You asked 20 people. All 20 said no. You feel defeated.

**❌ QUITTING:**  
*"Nobody wants to help. Let's just stop the project."*

**✅ PERSEVERANCE:**  
*"20 no's hurt, but we only need ONE yes. Let's try a different approach. Maybe online fundraising? Maybe asking businesses instead of individuals? We'll keep trying."*

## VALUE 7: HUMILITY & LEARNING

### WHAT IT MEANS

**Simple Definition:**  
Stay teachable. Admit mistakes. Listen to others. You don't know everything.

**Nigerian Translations:**
- **Pidgin:** "I fit learn from anybody."
- **Hausa:** "Tawali'u da koyi."
- **Yoruba:** "Ìrẹ̀lẹ̀ àti kíkọ́."
- **Igbo:** "Obi umeala na ịmụta ihe."

### WHY IT MATTERS

**Pride destroys. Humility builds.**

The person who thinks they know everything stops learning. The person who stays humble keeps growing.

In Nigeria, we see pride everywhere:
- Leaders who won't admit mistakes
- Adults who won't learn from youth
- Educated people who look down on those without degrees
- Rich people who think they're better than poor people

**This pride is killing our nation.**

National Builders are different. We are:
- Humble enough to learn from anyone
- Brave enough to admit when we're wrong
- Wise enough to know we don't know everything

### NIGERIAN EXAMPLE: PRIDE VS. HUMILITY

**THE PROUD PROJECT LEADER:**

Chike led a youth project to build a well. Community elders said, "The ground there is too rocky. Dig somewhere else."

Chike thought: *"Old people don't know anything. I studied engineering."*

He ignored their advice. His team spent 3 weeks digging. They hit solid rock. The project failed. Money and time wasted.

**THE HUMBLE PROJECT LEADER:**

Ada led a similar project. Elders gave the same advice.

Ada said: *"Thank you. You've lived here 60 years. You know this land better than me. Where should we dig?"*

They showed her a better spot. Her well was completed in 1 week and still works today.

**The difference?** Humility to learn from others.

### HUMILITY IN EVERYDAY LIFE

#### EXAMPLE 1: The Wrong Answer

You confidently answer a question in class. The teacher says you're wrong. Everyone heard.

**❌ PRIDE:**  
Get defensive. Argue. Blame the teacher. Feel embarrassed.

**✅ HUMILITY:**  
*"Thank you for correcting me. I misunderstood. What's the right answer?"*

---

#### EXAMPLE 2: The Better Idea

Your teammate suggests a different approach to the project. Their idea is better than yours.

**❌ PRIDE:**  
Reject it because you want your idea to win. *"I'm the leader. We're doing it my way."*

**✅ HUMILITY:**  
*"You're right. Your idea is better than mine. Let's use it. Thank you!"*

### HUMILITY IN YOUR PROJECT

#### COLLABORATIVE LEADERSHIP

As a National Builder, practice humble leadership:

✅ **ASK, DON'T TELL**  
Instead of: *"Do it this way."*  
Say: *"What do you think is the best approach?"*

✅ **CREDIT OTHERS**  
When someone contributes, say their name:  
*"This idea was Fatima's."*  
*"Ahmed did the hard work on this part."*

✅ **ADMIT WHEN YOU DON'T KNOW**  
It's okay to say: *"I don't know, but let's find out together."*

✅ **INVITE FEEDBACK**  
Ask your team: *"What am I doing well? What could I do better?"*

✅ **LEARN FROM BENEFICIARIES**  
Don't assume you know their problems. Ask them:  
*"What do you really need?"*  
*"How can we make this better for you?"*

## VALUE 8: EXCELLENCE & CRAFTSMANSHIP

### WHAT IT MEANS

**Simple Definition:**  
Do quality work. Pay attention to details. Take pride in what you create. Always improve.

**Nigerian Translations:**
- **Pidgin:** "If I wan do am, make I do am correct."
- **Hausa:** "Kyau da ƙwarewa."
- **Yoruba:** "Ipò gíga àti ọgbọ́n ọnà."
- **Igbo:** "Ịdi mma na nka."

### WHY IT MATTERS

**Nigeria has a reputation problem:**
- "Made in Nigeria" is seen as low quality
- Nigerian contractors do shoddy work
- Students cut corners on assignments
- Professionals deliver mediocre service

**This is NOT because Nigerians lack talent. It's because we've lost the culture of EXCELLENCE.**

We've accepted:
- "Good enough"
- "At least I tried"
- "Nobody will notice"
- "That's how everyone does it"

**National Builders refuse to accept mediocrity. We demand excellence from ourselves.**

### WHAT IS EXCELLENCE?

Excellence is:
- ✅ Doing your absolute best, not just the minimum
- ✅ Quality over speed
- ✅ Attention to detail - the small things matter
- ✅ Pride in your work - would you sign your name on it?
- ✅ Continuous improvement - always learning to do better

Excellence is NOT:
- ❌ Perfection (impossible)
- ❌ Being the best (comparison with others)
- ❌ Expensive (you can do excellent work with simple materials)

### NIGERIAN HEROES WHO SHOWED EXCELLENCE

#### 1. PHILIP EMEAGWALI

Nigerian computer scientist. Despite limited resources, he pushed himself to excellence. He taught himself advanced mathematics. He won the Gordon Bell Prize (supercomputing's Nobel Prize).

His excellence brought honor to Nigeria globally.

#### 2. BEN ENWONWU

Nigerian artist whose paintings are worth millions today. He could have done quick, simple art. Instead, he spent months on single pieces, perfecting every detail.

His excellent craftsmanship made Nigerian art respected worldwide.

### EXCELLENCE IN EVERYDAY LIFE

#### EXAMPLE 1: The School Assignment

Your teacher assigns a project. Minimum requirement: 5 pages.

**❌ MEDIOCRITY:**  
Write exactly 5 pages. Copy from Google. Don't check for errors. Submit.

**✅ EXCELLENCE:**  
Research thoroughly. Write 7 pages because the topic deserves it. Check grammar. Make it look professional. Add original insights.

---

#### EXAMPLE 2: The Community Cleanup

Your team volunteers to clean a drainage. The community expects you to work for 2 hours.

**❌ MEDIOCRITY:**  
Clean just the visible surface. Leave at exactly 2 hours even if not finished. Take lots of photos for social media.

**✅ EXCELLENCE:**  
Clean thoroughly, including hidden areas. Stay an extra 30 minutes to finish properly. Take few photos - the work speaks for itself.

#### THE EXCELLENCE CHECKLIST

Before submitting ANY work for your project, check:

☐ **ACCURATE** - Are my facts and numbers correct?  
☐ **CLEAR** - Can someone else easily understand this?
