## INTRODUCTION: WHY MONTHLY CHECK-INS MATTER

### Welcome to Your Progress Tracker!

This workbook helps you stay on track during your 6-month project journey (May-October).

**Why monthly check-ins are important:**

✅ **ACCOUNTABILITY** - You commit to making progress each month  
✅ **COURSE CORRECTION** - Catch problems early before they become big  
✅ **DOCUMENTATION** - Record your journey for the final report  
✅ **MOTIVATION** - See your progress, celebrate wins  
✅ **LEARNING** - Reflect on what's working and what's not  

### What Happens If You Don't Track Progress?

Many projects fail because:
- Teams lose momentum after the first month
- Small problems become big crises
- No one remembers what they did (poor documentation)
- Team members disagree about who did what
- October arrives and there's nothing to report

**Don't let this happen to you!**

Monthly check-ins take only 30-60 minutes but keep your project alive.

## PHOTO DOCUMENTATION GUIDE

### WHY PHOTOS MATTER

Photos are your PROOF that:
- The problem exists
- You did the work
- You made an impact
- Real people benefited

**Without photos, your project story is much weaker.**

### WHAT TO PHOTOGRAPH

**EVERY MONTH, TAKE PHOTOS OF:**

✅ **Your team working** (show action, not just posing)  
✅ **The people you're helping** (with their permission)  
✅ **Before/during/after** of the problem being solved  
✅ **Materials and resources** you're using  
✅ **Community members participating**  
✅ **Any challenges** you're facing  
✅ **Small wins and milestones**  

### PHOTO TIPS FOR SMARTPHONES

**1. LIGHTING:**
- Shoot during daytime when possible
- Avoid harsh midday sun (early morning or late afternoon is best)
- If indoors, stand near windows

**2. COMPOSITION:**
- Get close to your subject
- Include people's faces (shows human impact)
- Show context (where is this happening?)
- Take multiple shots of the same thing

**3. TECHNICAL:**
- Hold phone steady (lean against something if needed)
- Clean your camera lens before shooting
- Use the gridlines on your camera to center subjects
- Don't use digital zoom (step closer instead)

**4. STORYTELLING:**
- Capture emotions (smiles, concentration, teamwork)
- Show the scale (how big is the problem/solution?)
- Document the process, not just the result

### HOW MANY PHOTOS?

**Minimum:** 10 photos per month  
**Recommended:** 20-30 photos per month  
**Best practice:** Take 50+ photos, choose the best 20

### PHOTO STORAGE

**Organize your photos by month:**

Create folders:
- 📁 NBC_Project_May_2025
- 📁 NBC_Project_June_2025
- 📁 NBC_Project_July_2025
- etc.

**Backup your photos:**
- Upload to Google Drive/OneDrive
- Send to mentor
- Keep on multiple devices

**Don't lose your photos!** They're essential for your final report.

### GETTING PERMISSION

**Before photographing people:**

1. Explain you're doing a National Builders project
2. Ask: "May I take your photo to show the impact of our work?"
3. If they say no, respect it
4. For children, ask parents/teachers first

### PHOTO CAPTIONS

For each photo, write a brief caption:

**Format:**  
*"[What's happening] in [location], [date]. [Impact/significance]."*

**Example:**  
*"Ada teaching 10 children to read at Mushin Primary School, June 15, 2025. These children have improved their reading by 2 grade levels in one month."*

## PROBLEM-SOLVING WHEN YOU'RE STUCK

### COMMON PROBLEMS & SOLUTIONS

---

### PROBLEM 1: We're running out of money

**SOLUTIONS:**

☐ **Get creative with free/cheap alternatives**  
What can you use that costs nothing?

☐ **Ask for in-kind donations**  
What can people donate (services, materials) instead of money?

☐ **Organize a small fundraiser**  
What can you sell or do to raise ₦5,000-10,000?

☐ **Simplify your approach**  
What's the minimum version that still works?

☐ **Partner with a local business**  
What business might sponsor you for publicity?

---

### PROBLEM 2: Team members aren't showing up

**SOLUTIONS:**

☐ **Have a direct conversation**  
"We need you. What's stopping you from participating?"

☐ **Adjust meeting times**  
Maybe the schedule doesn't work for everyone?

☐ **Clarify expectations**  
Does everyone know what's expected of them?

☐ **Make it more engaging**  
Is the work boring? How can you make it fun?

☐ **Last resort: Replace them**  
If they won't commit, find someone who will

---

### PROBLEM 3: The community isn't participating

**SOLUTIONS:**

☐ **Ask them why**  
"What would make you more interested in this project?"

☐ **Involve them in planning**  
Did you design this FOR them or WITH them?

☐ **Show early wins**  
Even small successes can build momentum

☐ **Find a community champion**  
One respected person can influence many others

☐ **Address their concerns**  
Are they worried about something?

---

### PROBLEM 4: Our solution isn't working

**SOLUTIONS:**

☐ **Diagnose the problem**  
What specifically isn't working? Why?

☐ **Ask for expert advice**  
Who has solved this problem before?

☐ **Test a different approach**  
What's Plan B?

☐ **Adjust expectations**  
Maybe you need to solve a smaller piece first?

☐ **Learn and pivot**  
It's okay to change your strategy

---

### PROBLEM 5: We're exhausted and want to quit

**SOLUTIONS:**

☐ **Take a short break**  
Rest for a few days, then return refreshed

☐ **Remember your WHY**  
Why did you start this? Read your Values Commitment

☐ **Celebrate what you've done**  
Don't focus only on what's left

☐ **Ask for help**  
Call your mentor or coordinator

☐ **Adjust the pace**  
Maybe you're trying to do too much too fast?

☐ **Lean on your team**  
Talk about how everyone is feeling

---

### PROBLEM 6: We have a conflict in the team

**SOLUTIONS:**

☐ **Address it immediately**  
Don't let resentment build

☐ **Have a team meeting with ground rules:**
- Everyone gets to speak
- Listen without interrupting
- Focus on the problem, not the person
- Find a solution together

☐ **Get a mediator**  
Ask your mentor to help facilitate

☐ **Clarify roles**  
Sometimes conflict comes from confusion about who does what

☐ **Practice the value of HUMILITY**  
Apologize if you were wrong. Forgive if others were.

---

### WHEN TO ASK FOR HELP

**Contact your regional coordinator if:**

☐ You're stuck and can't figure out what to do  
☐ You haven't made progress in 2 weeks  
☐ Team conflict is affecting the project  
☐ You need resources you can't get  
☐ Community is hostile to your project  
☐ Safety concerns arise  
☐ You're considering quitting  

**Don't suffer in silence. NBC is here to support you!**

## CELEBRATING SMALL WINS

### WHY CELEBRATION MATTERS

Projects are HARD. If you only celebrate at the end, you'll lose motivation.

**Celebrate small wins along the way!**

### WHAT TO CELEBRATE

**EVERY MONTH, celebrate:**

✅ Completing a major milestone  
✅ First person/family helped  
✅ Overcoming a big challenge  
✅ Receiving a donation or partnership  
✅ Positive community feedback  
✅ Learning a new skill  
✅ Staying consistent (worked every week)  
✅ Good teamwork moments  

### HOW TO CELEBRATE

**Free celebrations:**
- Team high-fives or group hug
- Take a fun team photo
- Share your win on social media
- Thank each other verbally
- Write down the win in your journal

**Low-cost celebrations:**
- Share snacks together (₦500)
- Team outing to a local spot (₦1,000-2,000)
- Make certificates for each other
- Create a "Wins Wall" with photos

**Remember:** Celebration fuels motivation for next month!
