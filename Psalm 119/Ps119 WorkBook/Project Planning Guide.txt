## INTRODUCTION: WHY PLANNING MATTERS

### Welcome to Project Planning!

Many people have great ideas but poor execution. Why? Because they skip planning.

**The difference between:**
- A dream and a goal = A written plan
- An idea and a project = An action plan
- Hope and results = Measurement plan

**"Failing to plan is planning to fail."**

This workbook will guide you step-by-step to create a solid project plan. Don't rush through it. Take your time. A good plan saves you months of wasted effort.

### HOW TO USE THIS WORKBOOK

1. **Read each section carefully**
2. **Answer every question honestly**
3. **Discuss your answers with your team/mentor**
4. **Revise your plan as you learn more**
5. **Keep this workbook handy** - refer to it throughout your project

### WHAT MAKES A GOOD PROJECT PLAN?

A good plan is:
- ✅ **SPECIFIC** - Clear details, not vague ideas
- ✅ **REALISTIC** - You can actually do it with available resources
- ✅ **MEASURABLE** - You can prove your impact with numbers
- ✅ **TIME-BOUND** - Has deadlines and milestones
- ✅ **FLEXIBLE** - Can adapt when things change

**Let's begin planning your nation-building project!**

## STEP 1: UNDERSTAND THE PROBLEM

### WHY THIS MATTERS

You can't solve a problem you don't understand. Many projects fail because they address symptoms, not root causes.

**Example:**
- **SYMPTOM:** Children are failing exams
- **ROOT CAUSE:** They can't read well (poor literacy in early grades)

If you only focus on exam prep, you won't solve the real problem. You must dig deeper.

### 1.1 DESCRIBE THE PROBLEM

- "Children in my community don't have clean water to drink"
- "Youth in my area have no job skills and are unemployed"
- "Our school has no library, so students can't practice reading"

**Bad examples (too vague):**
- "Education is bad" (What exactly is bad about it?)
- "The community needs help" (What kind of help?)

## SAMPLE PROJECT EXAMPLES

### EXAMPLE 1: COMMUNITY LIBRARY PROJECT

**Problem:** Primary school has 500 students but no library. Children can't practice reading.

**Solution:** Create a simple community library using donated books and local volunteers.

**Action Plan:**
- May: Collect 200 books through donation drive
- June: Build simple bookshelves from recycled materials
- July: Train 5 volunteer librarians from the community
- August-September: Open library 3 days per week, track usage
- October: Measure reading improvement in students

**Resources:**
- Donated books from churches, NGOs, individual homes
- Recycled wood for shelves (from local carpenter)
- Volunteer time from parents and retired teachers
- Classroom space donated by school

**Impact Measurement:**
- Number of books collected
- Number of children using library weekly
- Reading test scores (before/after)
- Parent testimonials

**Sustainability:**
- Parent-Teacher Association will manage library
- Monthly book donations from community
- Volunteer rotation schedule created

**Estimated Cost:** ₦15,000 (for paint, nails, labels, registration system)

---

### EXAMPLE 2: WASTE RECYCLING INITIATIVE

**Problem:** Market area has no waste management. Garbage piles up, causing health problems and flooding.

**Solution:** Organize weekly cleanup + recycling system where plastic bottles are collected and sold.

**Action Plan:**
- May: Map waste hotspots, meet with market leaders
- June: Recruit 10 youth volunteers, get recycling company contact
- July-August: Weekly cleanups every Saturday morning
- September: Create permanent waste collection points
- October: Measure waste reduced, income generated from selling recyclables

**Resources:**
- Volunteer youth labor (no cost)
- Donated gloves and bags from local businesses
- Partnership with recycling company (buys plastic)
- Market association provides storage space

**Impact Measurement:**
- Tons of waste collected each month
- Number of volunteers participating
- Money earned from recycling
- Flood incidents reduced (compare to last year)
- Before/after photos of market cleanliness

**Sustainability:**
- Money from selling recyclables funds ongoing operations
- Youth volunteers rotate, train new people
- Market association incorporates into regular maintenance

**Estimated Cost:** ₦8,000 (initial supplies, transportation for first month)

---

### EXAMPLE 3: YOUTH SKILLS TRAINING

**Problem:** Many youth ages 16-25 in community are unemployed with no marketable skills.

**Solution:** Weekly vocational training in tailoring, phone repair, and hairdressing using local experts.

**Action Plan:**
- May: Survey youth needs, recruit 3 skilled trainers from community
- June: Enroll 30 youth, begin Saturday training sessions
- July-September: Weekly 2-hour sessions, each person learns one skill
- October: Graduation ceremony, help youth start micro-businesses

**Resources:**
- Volunteer trainers (experienced tailors, phone technicians, hairdressers)
- Community center space (free)
- Used sewing machines (borrowed/donated)
- Startup kits (small tools) donated by local businesses

**Impact Measurement:**
- Number of youth trained
- Number who start earning income
- Testimonials about life change
- Skills tests before/after training

**Sustainability:**
- Graduated students become trainers for next cohort
- Students pay small fee (₦500) if they can afford, creating fund for materials
- Partner businesses hire graduates

**Estimated Cost:** ₦25,000 (materials, certificates, startup kits for 5 top performers)

## COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

### MISTAKE 1: Planning alone without community input

**Why it's bad:** Your solution might not match what people actually need.

**How to avoid:** Interview at least 10 community members before finalizing your plan.

---

### MISTAKE 2: Over-ambitious plans you can't finish

**Why it's bad:** Better to do one thing excellently than five things poorly.

**How to avoid:** Be realistic. Can you actually do this in 6 months with your resources?

---

### MISTAKE 3: No backup plan for when things go wrong

**Why it's bad:** Things WILL go wrong. If you haven't thought ahead, you'll panic and quit.

**How to avoid:** For every major risk, have a "Plan B" ready.

---

### MISTAKE 4: Forgetting to document as you go

**Why it's bad:** In October, you'll struggle to remember what you did. You'll have no proof.

**How to avoid:** Take photos EVERY time you work. Keep a simple journal.

---

### MISTAKE 5: Depending on one person for everything

**Why it's bad:** If that person gets sick or quits, your whole project collapses.

**How to avoid:** Distribute responsibilities. Train backups.

---

### MISTAKE 6: Ignoring advice from elders/experts

**Why it's bad:** They have experience you lack. Their wisdom could save you months of mistakes.

**How to avoid:** Regularly consult your advisor. Listen with humility.

---

### MISTAKE 7: No plan for what happens after October

**Why it's bad:** Your project dies the moment the competition ends. No lasting impact.

**How to avoid:** Build sustainability into your plan from Day 1.
